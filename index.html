PRIMARY DIRECTIVE

Making things has always been more than a hobby — it's a way of thinking. There's something deeply satisfying about starting with a spool of filament and ending up with something that makes a kid's eyes light up.

Josiah's Maker Cave was born from a passion for the craft and a love of seeing 3D printed toys bring real joy. Articulating dragons, fidget gadgets, puzzles, mythical creatures — if it can be imagined, the printer can probably attempt it.

MARKET RESEARCH PROTOCOL

Deciding which prints to bring to a craft fair involves a rigorous evaluation process. Candidate items are presented to two field experts — my daughters — and selections are made based on the intensity of their excitement response.

Polite interest means it stays home. Grabbing it and running means it's in the bag. This methodology has proven highly effective and is not subject to peer review.

EQUIPMENT & CAPABILITY
